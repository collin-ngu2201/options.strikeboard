Read this first. A long box pays exactly the strike width at expiry — a flat payoff, no market risk. But it costs ~the present value of that width, so the "guaranteed profit" is just interest (~the risk-free rate), and after crossing the bid/ask and fees it is usually negative. Any big positive edge on this screen is almost certainly a stale delayed quote you cannot fill. This scanner exists to prove that with your own data.

Criteria for a box worth trading
1. European-style, cash-settled underlyings only (SPX, XSP, NDX) — American-style options (SPY, AAPL…) can be assigned early, which breaks the "riskless" payoff.
2. Executable prices — judge at bid/ask, never mid; then try to work the order inside the spread.
3. All-in costs — 4 legs × fees × 2 if you exit early.
4. Compare the implied APR to T-bills — a good long box is a cash-management trade earning ~risk-free yield with SPAN margin efficiency, not a profit machine. If the APR beats your broker's cash rate, it can make sense as a T-bill substitute.
5. Verify on live quotes — this feed is ~15 min delayed; stale legs manufacture fake edges.
